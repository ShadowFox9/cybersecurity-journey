# Day 2 - Types of Cyber Attacks & Threats

## Malware
- Definition: Malware means malicious software — any software designed to harm, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to a system.
- Types: Virus — attaches itself to a file and spreads when that file is opened, Worm — spreads automatically across networks without needing a host file, Trojan — disguises itself as legitimate software but is malicious underneath
Ransomware — encrypts your files and demands payment to unlock them, Spyware — silently monitors your activity and steals information
- Real world example: stuxnet, emotet, olympic vision

## Phishing
- Definition: A social engineering attack where an attacker tricks you into revealing sensitive information — passwords, credit card numbers, etc — by pretending to be someone trustworthy like your bank or Google.
- Real world example: You get an email saying "Your account has been compromised, click here to reset your password" — the link takes you to a fake website that steals your credentials.

## Man in the Middle (MITM)
- Definition: The attacker secretly positions themselves between two communicating parties — intercepting and sometimes altering the communication without either party knowing.
- Real world example: You connect to a fake WiFi hotspot at a café. The attacker sees everything you send and receive.

## DoS & DDoS
- Definition: DoS (Denial of Service) — attacker floods a system with traffic to overwhelm it and make it unavailable
DDoS (Distributed DoS) — same thing but using thousands of compromised machines (a botnet) to attack at once, making it much harder to stop
- Difference between DoS and DDoS: DDoS makes use of thousands of compromised machines and it is much more harder to stop unlike DoS attack

## SQL Injection
- Definition: An attack on databases. The attacker injects malicious SQL code into an input field (like a login form) to manipulate the database.
- Real world example:  Instead of typing a username, an attacker types code that tricks the database into revealing all user passwords

## Social Engineering
- Definition: The art of manipulating people rather than systems to gain unauthorized access. Phishing is one type but social engineering is broader — it includes phone calls, impersonation, pretexting.
- Real world example:  Someone calls your IT department pretending to be a new employee and convinces them to reset a password.
