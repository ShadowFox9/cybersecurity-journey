## Biggest Takeaway From The Week
This week showed me that cybersecurity isn't just reading or knowing random stuff about how to protect systems, it's hands-on practice. I went from not knowing how to navigate Linux at all to confidently creating users, changing permissions, and even analyzing red flags in a phishing email. The moment that stuck with me most was realizing how easily attackers can disguise a malicious link to look legitimate and how easily i could've fallen for it had it been that i've not been introduced to cybersecurity and different threat actors and patterns, it made me realize how much trust we place in things just looking 'normal and also a how single command in linux can make or mar your work . I'm happy that I documented everything and pushed it to GitHub daily, because that's proof I'm not just learning, I'm building something real & worthwhile, i'm supper proud of myself not because i've mastered everything but i'm improving and with consistency and proper structured practice i'll getting better.
